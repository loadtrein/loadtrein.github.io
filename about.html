About

Dimitri Álvarez is a Spanish Computer Engineer ( University of Oviedo ( 2012 ) ) that has always been interested in computer games and graphics since he was a child.

He has been always looking to learn and gain technical knowledge, from choosing technical subjects at secondary education to making the decision to enter University and study a Computer Engineering BSc.

During the study of the BSc, he had contact with some of the techniques used in the rendering of computer graphics, but since they were not focused on computer games at all, he decided to develop a didactic video game as the BSc final dissertation to start gaining skills in this field.

Due to the fact that his academic and professional curiosity was still unsolved at the end of the BSc, he decided to course MSc Computer Games & Entertainment, a specific postgraduate masters course at Goldsmiths University of London.

In June 2014, he started working for West Pier Studio, a video games company based in Brighton (United Kingdom). He worked on the development of new features for DeckTools Inspire, studio's main project, mainly focused on procedural generation of content, tools, ui and gameplay programming.

Games have been his passion since he got a console at the age of nine, not just about their playable and fun part but also impressed by the graphic techniques with which they were created. He's interested in a wide range of games genres such as Sandbox, Shooters or Real-Time Strategy games.

He is also passionate about music, playing the electric guitar since he was 15 and attending as many gigs as he can. Travelling, watching films and practising any kind of sport are some of the activities he loves to do in his spare time.

He is a strongly motivated person seeking for new challenges that will give him the opportunity to continue learning.
