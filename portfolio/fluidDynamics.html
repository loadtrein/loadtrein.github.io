3D Fluid Dynamics

« Back to portfolio

Realistic fluid-like effects, such as rising smoke, clouds or the flow of rivers, are nowadays found as a common asset in cutting edge game engines since they help to immerse players into virtual worlds by adding these into games and films.

This demo is an extension to support 3D of Jos Stam's fluid dynamic algorithm based on the physical equations of fluid flow, namely the Navier-Stokes equations. It shows a simulation of the explosion of an atomic bomb and demonstrates the expansion of different sources with different colours.

The 3D representation is achieved by adding an extra velocity direction and dividing a cube into smaller cubes, each one which is own density. The density is affected by the sources that we add to the bigger cube.


Its key features are:

  • Extension of Jos Stam's fluid dynamic algorithm to support 3D
  • Simulation of the explosion of an atomic bomb with mushroom cloud
  • Expansion of different sources with different colours
  • Developed in C++ and OpenGL
